Anatomy

Giraffes anatomy is similar to other birds there size, prioritizing a low weight, and maximum wingspan. They have many specialized organs to help facilitate this, such as the 6 gas sacks hidden in their abdomens, which serve both as a source of bouyancy and as a self defence mechanism, as they explode violently when the giraffe is threathened. other adaptations include hollow bones long necks, for improved aerodynamics.
